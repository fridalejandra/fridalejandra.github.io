I am a PhD candidate in geography at UCLA, where I also completed my undergraduate studies.
My research focuses on Antarctic sea ice variability and more boradly, Earth’s climate system. Currently, I am working on a project to better understand the timing of sea ice advance and retreat to serve as a point for climate model diagnostics. 

For example, When we look at the annual cycle averaged over space, it looks coherent (left), yet when we look at the timing of sea ice retreat spatially, we see the the timing of retreat differs around the continent(right). My work seeks to understand what atmosphetric mechanisms result in the heteregeneity of Antarctic sea ice melt:
